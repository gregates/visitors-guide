Phinney Ridge

Overview

Phinney Ridge is a long, elevated bit of land that runs north-south through the city, dividing Green Lake and Wallingford from Ballard and Fremont. Phinney Ave., which turns into Greenwood Ave. as it continues north through the neighborhood, is the main arterial street running through the neighborhood. From that street at the top of the ridge, you can see both west to the Olympic Mountains on the peninsula, and east to the Cascades.

Phinney Ridge is bounded by:

  • NW Market St. and N 50th St. to the south,
  • NW 80th St. to the north,
  • 8th Ave. NW to the west,
  • and Aurora Ave./US 99 to the east,
each of which is an arterial street as well.

The neighborhood is mostly residential, with a commercial corridor along Phinney/Greenwood Ave. Phinney Ridge is also home to the Woodland Park Zoo.
